Dawn of the Bluegrass Era

The Beginning of it All

Follow the stories of Bill Monroe, Flatt & Scruggs, and many more pioneers as they formed the bedrock of this great American art form. In addition to items from Monroe’s estate, you can learn about Monroe’s early years and how he came to create this music. There is something very compelling here for the casually curious, seasoned fans, and day-trippers alike.






Flatt & Scruggs and the Foggy Mountain Boys was an American bluegrass band. The band was founded by guitarist Lester Flatt and banjo player Earl Scruggs and is viewed by music historians as one of the premier bluegrass groups in the history of the genre. The band was originally formed in 1948 by Flatt, who had been a member of Bill Monroe's bluegrass band. Flatt brought Scruggs with him shortly after leaving Monroe.






Radio stations dedicated to bluegrass have also proved influential in advancing the evolution of the style into distinctive subgenres. Bluegrass was initially included in the category of folk music and later changed to hillbilly. In 1948, bluegrass was placed under the country/western heading for radio airplay charting.
